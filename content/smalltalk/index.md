Since learning [Smalltalk](http://www.world.st) at KTH in around 1994 - we used VisualWorks 1.0 IIRC - I have been deeply in love with both the language, its mindset and the community with all its diversity. During many years I was very active in the [Squeak](http://squeak.org) community and I have also been using [Pharo](http://pharo.org) for various development tasks.

Today in 2019 I would say **Pharo is the leading open source Smalltalk dialect**, sharing lots of resources with its ancestor, Squeak, especially the [Cog VM](http://www.mirandabanda.org/cogblog/about-cog/), these days going under the name [OpenSmalltalk](http://www.opensmalltalk.org), that enables Pharo and Squeak to still be competitive alternatives.

The elegance of Smalltalk, through its careful design and very powerful toolset - still today makes it a compelling tool for many tasks. The [Design Principles Behind Smalltalk](/smalltalk/design-principles-behind-smalltalk.html)-article from Byte magazine 1981 is a must read for anyone seriously interested in programming languages.

> <strong>Good Design:</strong> A system should be built with a minimum set of unchangeable parts; those parts should be as general as possible; and all parts of the system should be held in a uniform framework.
> — Dan Ingalls, Byte magazine 1981

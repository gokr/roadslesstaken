I have followed [Dart](https://dart.dev) from the corner of my eye since the inception - after all, [Lars Bak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lars_Bak_(computer_programmer)), one of the creators is a very well known man in the Smalltalk/Self/OO world, so it had to be interesting!

In the beginning Dart lived a life away from the spotlight, trying to become a "better Javascript" sure is an impossible fight to pick. But with the birth of [Flutter](https://flutter.io) and the hard work to make Dart mature over the years, including moving to Dart 2.0 with a sound static type system that actually "feels nice" even for a Smalltalker - Dart is now an **extremely interesting platform.**

At work I am betting hard on Dart, not only for mobile apps via Flutter, but also server side using [Aqueduct](https://aqueduct.io) and a slew of [other packages](https://pub.dev).
